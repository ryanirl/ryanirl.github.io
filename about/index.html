About

I am a fourth-year honors Computer Science and Mathematics student at the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, and researcher at the Biosensing and Biorobotics Laboratory.

Additionally, I run the ML@UMN club, and will be a TA for CSCI 5980/8980 - Machine Learning for Health with Prof. Yoga Varatharajah in the Fall of 2024.

My research is largely focused on mechanistic interpretability and reverse engineering intelligent (learning) systems—both artificial and biological—to understand how complex behavior and cognition emerge at a mechanistic level. This research spans multiple disciplines (neuroscience, machine learning, and healthcare) and levels of organization, from circuits to emergent properties and higher-level theories of computation. My long-term goal is to apply these insights to advance AI safety, drug discovery, and treatments for neurological disorders.

Alongside theoretical work, I am also interested in developing efficient software tools to help study large datasets and models at scale. I am proficient in Python and most of its scientific computing libraries, though am interested to learn more about distributed computing, writing custom CUDA kernels, and high-performance machine learning.

When I'm not writing or doing research, you can likely find me at Minneapolis Bouldering Project, where I train ~10 hours a week.
